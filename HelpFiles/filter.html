Using the Dino Filter

The Dino Filer allow you to search through your recorded dinos based on certain criteria including: dino name, species, damage, etc.. It also allows you to quickly turn on and off your selected filter.

Turning the Filters On and Off

To start using the filters  click the "Toggle Filter" checkbox located right under the dino list. This turns on/off the master filter which enables/disables the application of filters to your dino list. (Note: when you turn off the master filter it preserves the individual filter settings allowing you to quickly toggle a set of filters on or off.)

To turn on/off the individual filters you click the check box next to the individual filters name.

Filtering by Name

One way you can filter your list of dinos by name (dino names, persons names and tribe names) to do this enter the name of the item you are searching for into the associated text box including any wildcard characters (Ark Notes supports the use of SQL wildcards in its filters). After entering the the name you check the check box next to the filter you want to enable and click the update filters button to refresh your dino list. 

Filtering By Species

Another filter method you can use to search yor dino list is by species. To do this click the dropdown box in the species filter and select the approprate choice from the list provided. after selecting your choice check the checkbox nexto the filter to enable it and click the update filters buton to refresh yor dino list with the selected filters.

Filtering by stats

In ark notes you can filter your dino list baced on the vairous stats of the dinos such as health, damage, and weight capacity to name a few. Ark Notes uses a rangebased system when filtering, this allows you to specify a minimum and/or maximum valye to limit your resaults.
